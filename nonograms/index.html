Nonograms

by Eric Savadian

How To Play

These are picture logic puzzles where cells in a grid must be filled or left blank according to the number hints at the side of the grid to reveal a picture. The numbers indicate the unbroken numbers of filled in squares in a row or column. For example, "5 3" means there sets of five and three squares in order with at least one blank space between them.

A great explanation of game strategies can be found by clicking here

Click a cell to toggle between Filled, X or Empty. X's are simple placeholders; I recommend using them on spaces you've ruled out, but they're completely optional and just for utility

When you click to toggle a box, you can also drag the mouse to draw the same type of box on multiple cells.

Keyboard controls are also available. You can use WASD, IJKL or Arrow keys to move around the board. Cells are toggleable with the Spacebar or Enter key.

Lastly, you can set a box to Filled with the Z or B keys, X with X or N and Empty with C or M.
